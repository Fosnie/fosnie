# Document skeletons

Clause-level skeletons for the documents this platform's users most often ask
for. These are structural checklists, not precedent text — draft the substance
for the matter at hand.

## Mutual NDA

1. Parties (both as discloser and recipient) · 2. Definitions (Confidential
Information — with the standard carve-outs: already known, independently
developed, public through no fault, received from a third party lawfully;
Purpose — drawn narrowly) · 3. Confidentiality obligations (use solely for the
Purpose; disclose only to representatives who need to know and are bound) ·
4. Compelled disclosure (notice, cooperation, minimum necessary) · 5. Return or
destruction on request (with a retention carve-out for legal/regulatory copies
and backups) · 6. No licence / no warranty as to accuracy · 7. Term of the
agreement and survival of confidentiality (typically 3–5 years; trade secrets
indefinitely) · 8. Remedies (damages inadequate; injunctive relief available) ·
9. Boilerplate (no assignment, entire agreement, variation in writing, notices,
governing law and jurisdiction) · Execution.

Watch for in third-party NDAs (triage flags): embedded non-solicit or
non-compete, missing carve-outs, one-way obligations dressed as mutual,
indemnities in what should be a plain NDA.

## Services / consultancy agreement

1. Parties · 2. Definitions · 3. Services (by reference to Schedule 1 —
description, deliverables, standards) · 4. Charges and payment (rates in
Schedule 2, invoicing cadence, payment terms, interest on late payment) ·
5. Term · 6. Supplier obligations (skill and care, compliance with laws and
policies) · 7. Client obligations (access, information, dependencies) ·
8. Personnel (key persons, substitution, no employment/IR35 posture as
applicable) · 9. Intellectual property (ownership of deliverables vs background
IP, licence back) · 10. Confidentiality · 11. Data protection (controller/
processor roles; processor terms or DPA reference where personal data is
processed) · 12. Liability (cap, exclusions, carve-outs: death/personal
injury, fraud) · 13. Insurance · 14. Termination (convenience with notice;
cause; insolvency) · 15. Consequences of termination (accrued rights, return of
materials, survival) · 16. Boilerplate · Schedules: 1 Services, 2 Charges ·
Execution.

## Variation agreement / deed of variation

1. Parties (as in the original) · 2. Background (recitals: date and title of
the original agreement; that the parties wish to vary it) · 3. Definitions
(the "Original Agreement") · 4. Variations — the surgical amendment formulas,
one per change: deleted-and-replaced / inserted / deleted, with the text quoted
in full · 5. No other changes (Original Agreement continues in full force as
varied) · 6. Effective date of the variations · 7. Counterparts, governing
law · Execution (as a deed if the original was a deed or no consideration).

## Engagement letter (professional services)

Addressee and date · Scope of the engagement (and expressly out of scope) ·
Deliverables and timetable · Team and responsibilities · Fees, expenses,
billing and payment terms · Client responsibilities and dependencies ·
Confidentiality and conflicts · Liability cap and basis · Complaints and
termination · Regulatory statements as applicable · Acceptance signature block
referencing attached standard terms.

## Board minutes

Company name and number · Meeting: date, time, place/format · Present /
in attendance / apologies · Quorum and chair noted · Declarations of interest ·
Business items, each as: background noted → documents produced → **IT WAS
RESOLVED THAT …** (resolutions in the exact operative wording) · Authority to
sign/file · Close · Signature of chair.
